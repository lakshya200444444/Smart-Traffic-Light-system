\chapter{Engineering Considerations}
Developing an AI-based traffic management system requires careful consideration of multiple engineering aspects to ensure that the solution is practical, reliable, and efficient in real-world environments.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Hardware Selection:}  
    Choosing appropriate hardware such as cameras, sensors, microcontrollers, and servers is critical for accurate data collection and system performance. Devices must be weather-resistant and capable of operating in various environmental conditions.

    \item \textbf{Data Processing and Storage:}  
    The system must handle large volumes of real-time data efficiently. This involves designing a robust data pipeline for collection, preprocessing, storage, and analysis. Cloud-based platforms may be considered for scalability.

    \item \textbf{AI Model Integration:}  
    Integrating machine learning models into the traffic control system requires proper training and validation using real or simulated traffic data. The models must be optimized for speed and accuracy to make timely decisions.

    \item \textbf{Power and Connectivity:}  
    Reliable power supply and uninterrupted internet connectivity are essential for continuous system operation. Backup systems (e.g., battery or solar power) may be required in case of outages.

    \item \textbf{Safety and Reliability:}  
    The system must be designed to handle failures gracefully. Fail-safes should be implemented to revert to manual control in case the AI system becomes unresponsive.

    \item \textbf{Scalability and Maintenance:}  
    The design should allow easy scaling to additional intersections or cities. Maintenance protocols need to be established for hardware servicing, software updates, and troubleshooting.

    \item \textbf{Environmental and Ethical Concerns:}  
    Consideration must be given to reducing the environmental impact of the system. Additionally, data privacy and ethical handling of surveillance data must be ensured.
\end{itemize}
