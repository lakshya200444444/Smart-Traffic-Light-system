\chapter{Literature Review}
Managing traffic in busy cities has become increasingly difficult due to the growing number of vehicles and limited road space. Traditional traffic control systems, which follow fixed schedules, are often not capable of handling real-time traffic conditions. This results in longer waiting times, unnecessary delays, and increased pollution. To solve these issues, researchers have been exploring the use of artificial intelligence (AI) in traffic management. Various studies suggest that AI can make traffic systems more efficient by allowing them to respond to live traffic data. For example, machine learning algorithms can be trained to understand traffic patterns and adjust signal timings based on the number of vehicles at an intersection. Some research also focuses on using image processing and computer vision to count vehicles through CCTV cameras. Other approaches involve the use of Internet of Things (IoT) devices and sensors to collect data from roads, which can then be analyzed by AI systems. These technologies have shown potential in improving traffic flow, reducing congestion, and even giving priority to emergency vehicles. Although many of these smart systems are still under development or tested in limited areas, they provide a strong foundation for future improvements. This project builds on those ideas to create a real-time, AI-powered traffic management system that can better handle modern traffic challenges.
